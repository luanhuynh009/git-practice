Hi, my name is Catarina and I have some advice for you!

Practice makes perfect!

Well duh, I know that.

I remember when I looked at my first line of code and thought, "Wth is does all this mean?" "How am I ever going to make this a legit career for myself?" What I quickly learned was that with coding, everything starts to sink in with practice. So take the time to do the bonus work, the extra work, to make sure you're comfortable with all the new skills and knowledge you're aquiring.

Don't be afraid to ask (or look) for help

"There's no possible way you're going to remember all these lines of codes and commands."

These were the words my IT husband so gently said to me one night as I threaten to pull out my hair in frustration. All I had wanted to do was code an entire landing page (my first, mind you) by memory (palm to the face). He assured that even in his seasoned IT career, there were plently of times where he needed to research, look up, and even reach out for help.

Don't feel like you need to perfect the code. There's a reason why cheat sheets exist, use them! And if your spouse is in IT, put them on retainer!

Enjoy the learning process

There aren't many times during our adulthood that we can sit down and actually learn something new or build a new skill. So, if you've given yourself this opportunity, make sure you're enjoying it and that you're gaining all you need from it!
