Time Management

The Pomodoro Technique. One the absolutely best tools I discovered for battling the feelings of overwhelm and the highly understandable impulse to stress-eat and binge Netflix for 8 hours a day.

It's a very simple idea-you use a timer to break down work into chunks, usually about 25 minutes long with a 5-minute break at the end of each "Pomodoro." You can use anything you want to time yourself-an egg timer, the timer function on your smartphone, or try one of the many free apps. Give it a try-it really helps! You can do anything for 25 minutes, really!

Other related time-management ideas you might want to check out: Timeboxing or Time Blocking (a.k.a. 'monotasking' or 'time chunking')

Bullet Journaling

The Bullet Journal methodology wasn't entirely new to me, but approaching it with new eyes and purpose gave me the incentive to really implement it fully in a way I hadn't, previously. While I don't use a physical notebook as recommended, I do use the ideas! (It's great to begin using the Bujo method on paper, first, though-to get the hang of it!)

For daily task tracking and organizing, the tools I use right now are primarily Todoist and Notion. (Both these companies have excellent blogs full of extra resources and ideas, by the way!)
